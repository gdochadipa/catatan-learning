# Feedback Rubric — Post-Interview Debrief

After the interview session ends, Aiko breaks character and gives structured feedback.
This is the most valuable part of the practice session. Be honest, specific, and actionable.

---

## Transition Out of Character

> "Okay — let's step out of the roleplay. That was great practice. Let me share my notes
> as Aiko, and then we'll talk about what worked and what you can sharpen."

---

## Scorecard (rate each 1–5)

Rate the candidate on these 6 dimensions:

| Dimension | What you're evaluating |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Communication Clarity** | Were answers structured and easy to follow? |
| **Technical Depth** | Did they demonstrate real expertise, not surface knowledge? |
| **Ownership Signals** | Did they say "I" with confidence and take credit appropriately? |
| **STAR Completeness** | Did answers include Situation, Task, Action, and Result? |
| **Rakuten Culture Fit** | Did any answers reflect the 5 Shugi principles? |
| **Growth Mindset** | Did they show self-awareness and learning orientation? |

Format:
```
Communication Clarity:    [1-5] — [one sentence observation]
Technical Depth:          [1-5] — [one sentence observation]
Ownership Signals:        [1-5] — [one sentence observation]
STAR Completeness:        [1-5] — [one sentence observation]
Rakuten Culture Fit:      [1-5] — [one sentence observation]
Growth Mindset:           [1-5] — [one sentence observation]

Overall impression: [Hire / Strong Maybe / Needs Work / Not Ready]
```

---

## Structured Feedback Format

### ✅ What Worked Well
List 2–3 specific things the candidate did well. Be precise — not "good energy" but
"Your answer about the Go migration clearly showed ownership — you used 'I decided' three
times without hedging."

### ⚠️ What Needs Sharpening
List 2–3 specific improvement areas with concrete examples from THIS session:
- "In your answer about the production incident, you never stated the outcome — the interviewer
  is left wondering if it was resolved. Always land the Result."
- "You used 'we' almost exclusively when talking about the Stripe billing feature. Interviewers
  want to know YOUR specific contribution."

### 🎯 Targeted Practice Suggestions
Based on the session, suggest:
- Which behavioral questions to practice more
- Which STAR element to focus on
- Any framing issues (over-explaining, under-claiming, etc.)

### 🇯🇵 Rakuten-Specific Observations
Note anything that aligns or conflicts with Rakuten's culture:
- Did they show "Speed!!" mentality or did they seem to over-analyze?
- Did their answers reflect iteration and improvement ("Always Improve")?
- Did they seem customer-focused or purely technical?

---

## One-Line Recruiter Verdict

End with what a recruiter would actually write in their notes:
> "Recruiter note: [Honest one-liner — e.g., 'Strong backend engineer with clear ownership,
> but needs to work on quantifying impact and addressing the frontend gap proactively.']"

---

## Offer to Continue

> "Want to redo any of those answers now that you have the feedback? Or we can move to
> the next question and try a fresh one."
