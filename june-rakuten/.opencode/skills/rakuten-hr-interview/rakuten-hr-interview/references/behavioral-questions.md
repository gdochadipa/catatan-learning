# Behavioral Question Bank

All questions follow STAR methodology. Questions are grouped by competency.
Pick 3–4 per session, ensuring coverage across at least 3 different competencies.

---

## Competency 1: Ownership & Initiative
*(Rakuten value: Passionately Professional)*

- "Tell me about a time you took ownership of something that wasn't technically your responsibility."
- "Describe a time you identified a problem before it became critical and what you did about it."
- "Tell me about a project where you had to push back on a decision from a senior engineer or manager. What happened?"
- "Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information. How did you approach it?"

**Strong answer signals:**
- Uses "I" not "we" for their own contributions
- Mentions specific outcomes and metrics
- Shows proactive behavior, not reactive

---

## Competency 2: Dealing with Ambiguity & Problem Solving
*(Rakuten value: Hypothesize – Practice – Validate)*

- "Tell me about the most technically complex problem you've solved. Walk me through how you approached it."
- "Describe a time you had to figure out something you'd never done before under a tight deadline."
- "Tell me about a time a technical solution you proposed didn't work. What did you do?"
- "Describe a situation where the requirements kept changing. How did you manage that?"

**Strong answer signals:**
- Structured thinking (breaks problem down)
- Shows iteration and learning
- Data-driven validation

---

## Competency 3: Collaboration & Communication
*(Rakuten value: Maximize Customer Satisfaction — internal customers = teammates)*

- "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a team member or stakeholder. How was it resolved?"
- "Describe a time you had to explain a complex technical concept to a non-technical audience."
- "Tell me about a situation where you had to collaborate across teams or time zones."
- "Describe a time you gave difficult feedback to a colleague. How did you handle it?"

**Strong answer signals:**
- Demonstrates empathy without avoiding conflict
- Shows respect for others' perspectives
- Outcome-focused resolution

---

## Competency 4: Delivery Under Pressure
*(Rakuten value: Speed!! Speed!! Speed!!)*

- "Tell me about a time you had to deliver something important under extreme time pressure."
- "Describe a production incident you were involved in. What was your role and what happened?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to prioritize between multiple urgent tasks. How did you decide?"
- "Describe a time a project you were responsible for was at risk of missing a deadline."

**Strong answer signals:**
- Calm, structured response to pressure
- Clear decision-making about tradeoffs
- Post-incident learning and prevention

---

## Competency 5: Continuous Improvement
*(Rakuten value: Always Improve, Always Advance)*

- "Tell me about something significant you taught yourself in the last 12 months. Why that, and how?"
- "Describe a time you introduced a better way of doing something in your team."
- "Tell me about a piece of feedback you received that was hard to hear. What did you do with it?"
- "Describe a technical decision you regret. What would you do differently?"

**Strong answer signals:**
- Specific, not generic ("I always learn")
- Shows humility and adaptability
- Connects learning to real impact

---

## Competency 6: Technical Depth (for SDE roles)
*(Hybrid behavioral/technical)*

- "Walk me through the most interesting architectural decision you've made and why."
- "Tell me about a time you had to optimize for performance or scalability. What was the approach?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to balance technical debt against feature delivery."
- "Tell me about a time you made a system more reliable or observable. What did you change?"

**Strong answer signals:**
- Mentions tradeoffs explicitly
- Shows awareness of second-order effects
- Can quantify improvement

---

## Follow-Up Cheat Sheet

After any answer, Aiko can use these follow-ups:

| Situation | Follow-Up |
|-----------|-----------|
| Answer is too short | "Can you give me a bit more detail on what you personally did?" |
| Answer lacks numbers | "How did you measure whether it was successful?" |
| Outcome is vague | "What was the actual business impact?" |
| Answer uses 'we' throughout | "What was YOUR specific role in that?" |
| Answer is too theoretical | "Can you give me a concrete example?" |
| Strong answer | "That's great. What would you do differently today?" |
| Red flag spotted | "That's interesting — can you tell me more about what led to that situation?" |
