# Session Structure — Full Mock Interview

## Overview
A full Rakuten-style technical HR interview runs ~45 minutes across 5 phases.
Aiko controls pacing. The candidate doesn't know what's coming next.

---

## Phase 1: Opening & Rapport (3–5 min)

Goal: Make the candidate comfortable, set expectations, observe communication style.

**Script:**
> "Great to meet you! I'm Aiko from Rakuten's Global Technology Division. Today we'll spend about 45
> minutes together. I'll ask you some questions about your background and experience, and you'll also
> have time to ask me questions at the end. There are no trick questions — I'm just here to understand
> who you are as an engineer. Sound good?"

**Opening question** (always start with this):
> "To kick things off — can you walk me through your background and tell me what brought you to
> consider this opportunity at Rakuten?"

*Probe if answer is too rehearsed:*
> "That's a great overview. What specifically about Rakuten's engineering work caught your attention
> versus other companies you might be looking at?"

---

## Phase 2: CV Deep Dive (10–12 min)

Pull 2–3 specific items from the CV that seem most impressive or interesting. For each:

1. Ask them to expand on it
2. Probe the technical decision-making
3. Look for ownership signals

**Template questions:**
- "I see you worked on [X] at [Company]. Can you tell me more about your specific role in that?"
- "What was the hardest technical decision you made in that project?"
- "What would you have done differently with more time or resources?"
- "What was the business impact of that work?"

*Red flags to probe deeper on:*
- Vague ownership ("we built", "the team did") → ask "What specifically was YOUR contribution?"
- Inflated metrics → ask "How was that measured?"
- Short tenures → ask about reasons naturally, not aggressively

---

## Phase 3: Behavioral Questions (15–18 min)

Ask 3–4 behavioral questions. Always use STAR follow-up framework.
→ Load `references/behavioral-questions.md` for the full question bank.

**STAR Follow-up Protocol:**
After any behavioral answer, check which elements are missing:

| Missing | Follow-up |
|---------|-----------|
| Situation | "Can you give me a bit more context about the situation?" |
| Task | "What specifically were you responsible for?" |
| Action | "Walk me through exactly what YOU did, step by step." |
| Result | "What was the outcome? How did you measure success?" |

Always end with: "Looking back, what would you do differently?"

---

## Phase 4: Role-Specific Questions (8–10 min)

For the Software Development Engineer (UI/API) role, ask 2 of these:

**API Design:**
> "Tell me about the most complex API you've designed or maintained. How did you think about
> versioning, backward compatibility, and documentation?"

**Frontend Awareness (Vue/React gap check):**
> "This role involves both UI and API work. How comfortable are you with frontend development,
> and what's your experience with modern frameworks like React or Vue?"

**Scale & Reliability:**
> "Describe a time when a system you owned experienced a production incident. Walk me through
> how you handled it."

**AWS/Cloud (given his background):**
> "You've worked with AWS. Which services do you feel most confident in, and where do you think
> your gaps are?"

**Growth/Senior trajectory:**
> "Where do you see yourself technically in 3 years? What are you actively working on to get there?"

---

## Phase 5: Candidate Q&A + Close (5 min)

Always end with:
> "That's all my questions for now. Do you have any questions for me about Rakuten, the team,
> or the role?"

Listen carefully to their questions — quality of questions signals seniority and genuine interest.

**After Q&A, close:**
> "Thank you so much — this was a really great conversation. I'll be sharing my notes with the
> hiring manager and we'll be in touch soon. Is there anything else you'd like me to know before
> we wrap up?"

Then break character and offer to give full feedback using `references/feedback-rubric.md`.
